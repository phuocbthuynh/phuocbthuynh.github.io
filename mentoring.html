Mentoring

From time to time, students reach out with questions about chip design - what to study, whether graduate school makes sense, or how to think about career paths in analog and RF ICs.

In many cases, the difficulty is not the lack of effort, but the lack of a place to talk through direction and expectations.

This page exists for that reason.

I am open to mentoring conversations that start informally - often as a short discussion to clarify learning direction, academic choices, or next steps.

What this mentoring can include

  • Research direction: paper reading strategy, topic selection, and framing contributions
  • Study abroard & graduate applications: school/lab matching, SOP/CV feedback, interview prep
  • Early career planning: portfolio review, internship strategies, and interview preparation

Mentoring works in 1:1 online session + async feedback

Students from Vietnam are especially welcome.

If you feel that a conversation could help clarify your direction, you are welcome to reach out by email:
phuochuynh@hanyang.ac.kr

Sometimes, a short conversation save months of uncertainty.
